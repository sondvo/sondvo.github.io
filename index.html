About

I'm a PhD student in Cellular & Molecular Biology at Arizona State University and the Translational Genomics Research Institute (TGen), advised by Nicholas Banovich, Ph.D. My work sits at the intersection of spatial transcriptomics, single-cell genomics, and machine learning — building scalable analysis pipelines (often from scratch) and using deep learning to make sense of datasets exceeding 100 million cells.

Before my PhD I was a Biomedical Research Scientist at BioTuring, where I led computational collaborations with the NIH, Vertex, the Melton Lab at Harvard, and the University of Calgary, and helped build software now used by 70+ pharmaceutical companies and research institutes.
